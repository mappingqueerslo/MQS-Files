Cal Poly Pride Center

 Julian A. McPhee University Union, South Poly View Drive, San Luis Obispo, CA, USA
The door to the Cal Poly Pride Center showcasing pride colors. Photo taken for Mustang News May 2022.
The crosswalk outside Cal Poly's University Union is painted to represent the pride flag. Photograph courtesy of Cal Poly ASI.

The Pride Center on Cal Poly's campus has gone through many name and location changes since in inception in 1976 as the Gay Student Union. Currently it can be found in the University Union at (UU-217). The center provides resources for queer students as well as a community space for students to gather and socialize with each other.

2002

Pride Alliance Center is opened in a physical space off the main campus.

2000

Cal Poly celebrates its first Pride Week.

1993

AIDS Memorial Quilt is showcased at the Cal Poly Recreation Center.

1976

Club status is granted to the GSU.

1972

Students begin petitioning for Gay Student Union to be formed.

Timeline of events surrounding the Pride Center. Information provided by the Cal Poly Pride Center website.

The AIDS Memorial Quilt is an ongoing community project that began in 1985. Each quilt square is dedicated to someone who passed away as the result of AIDS and its related medical complications.
