Journey's Inn

 2115 Broad Street, San Luis Obispo, CA 93401
Article from April 12, 1985 edition of Mustang Daily discussing the gay bar.
Summer Mustang article from 1976 talking about the student presence at the bar.

Journey's Inn was the first gay bar in San Luis Obispo and was located at 2115 Broad Street. It is unclear what years the bar was operational, but news articles about the bar from the SLO Telegram-Tribune and Mustang Daily date between 1976 and 1988. The bar was a gathering place for gay and lesbian people from all over SLO County, but did not advertise itself publicly until the 80s for fear of discrimination. It was also a popular destination for Cal Poly students due to its cheap deals on food and drinks.

There is not much public information available about the history of Journey's Inn, but from what is available the bar seemed to be a laid-back and casual place where queer people could meet and socialize with each other. There was also a large crowd of straight customers and college students, so it was really just a place to mingle, unwind, and have some cheap drinks and food.
